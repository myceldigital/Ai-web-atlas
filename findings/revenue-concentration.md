# Finding: the mesh hides concentration

Logo maps make ecosystems look evenly distributed. Early-stage and growth-stage enterprise revenue rarely behaves that way.

A company can display a broad customer mesh while revenue remains concentrated in a handful of accounts. The map may be true as a list of logos and misleading as an implied commercial distribution.

## Risk

Readers may infer:

- broad monetization where there is mainly experimentation;
- equal commercial value across logos;
- category control from visual density;
- adoption from usage rather than paid deployment.

## Atlas treatment

AI Web Atlas treats logo presence as ecosystem evidence, not proof of revenue scale. Where relationship or materiality is unproven, the data should remain `probable_customer`, `category_marker`, or `uncertain` rather than `customer`.
